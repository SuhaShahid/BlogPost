Exploring Hidden Gems in Japan

Published on March 6, 2025 by Sarah Chen

Japan is a country of contrasts, where ancient traditions meet cutting-edge technology. During my recent three-week journey through the Land of the Rising Sun, I discovered some remarkable places that aren't on the typical tourist itinerary. From secluded mountain temples to local food markets, these hidden gems provided an authentic glimpse into Japanese culture.

Quick Trip Overview

My journey took me from Tokyo to Kyoto, then to the less-visited areas of Takayama, the Kii Peninsula, and finally to the art island of Naoshima. I spent 21 days exploring, using a mix of bullet trains, local buses, and occasionally renting bicycles to cover the more remote areas.
